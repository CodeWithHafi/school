Striving for the Highest

A Catholic minority institution dedicated to character formation, academic excellence, and self-discipline since its inception.

Our Vision

To inspire and nurture the human spirit to "Strive for the Highest" in all spheres of life, integrating love, wisdom, and service for the global world.

Our Mission

To prepare and motivate students for a fast-changing world by instilling a sense of belonging, justice, and compassion through a balanced curriculum.

Our Values

Creativity, Tolerance, Inclusion, Humility, and Integrity. We promote accountability in all that we do, teach, and demonstrate.

St. Eugene de Mazenod

Born in Aix-en-Provence, France (1782), St. Eugene was a Bishop and founder of the religious congregation Oblates of Mary Immaculate (OMI). His life was dedicated to reviving faith among the rural and industrial populations after the French Revolution.

Today, his followers work globally to help people develop their faith and improve their lives, a legacy that continues through our school.

General Rules of Discipline

  • Students must speak only English within school premises.
  • Clean and tidy school uniforms must be worn daily.
  • Use of mobile phones, electronic gadgets, or ornaments is strictly forbidden.
  • Irregular attendance or habitual idleness leads to dismissal.
  • Any damage to school property must be compensated by the student.

Notes to Parents

Parents are requested to collaborate with school authorities for character formation and discipline. Any communication must be addressed to the Principal.
